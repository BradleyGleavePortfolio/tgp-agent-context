# What Gym Owners & Front Desk Staff Want From Software
### A Research Brief for TGP Fitness Product Development
*Based on 3,300+ verified user reviews across 243 gym management products — G2 & Capterra, June 2026*

---

# PART 1: WHAT GYM OWNERS WANT

## Overview

Gym owners are the primary buyers of gym management software. They are almost always small business operators — 89% of all reviewers across 243 products on G2 fall into the "small business" category, meaning they run gyms with fewer than 50 employees and often wear every hat: operator, marketer, accountant, and head coach. Their relationship with software is deeply personal — a bad tool doesn't just slow them down, it costs them money, members, and peace of mind.

The single most common theme across positive and negative reviews alike is this: **gym owners want software that removes friction, not adds it.** They are not looking for the most feature-rich platform — they are looking for the platform that gets out of their way and lets them run their gym.

---

## 1. Revenue Transparency and Billing Control

This is the #1 pain point that drives gym owners away from platforms. Owners need to know exactly what money is coming in, what is failing, and why — in real time, not at the end of the month.

**What they want:**
- Automated recurring billing that runs without manual intervention
- Immediate visibility into failed payments with one-click retry
- Prorated billing when members start mid-cycle — this should be calculated automatically, not manually
- Flexible plan structures: monthly, annual, session packs, drop-ins, and hybrid combinations
- The ability to freeze or pause memberships without cancelling them (e.g., for injuries or travel)
- A clear, itemized breakdown of gross revenue, refunds, chargebacks, and platform fees on one screen
- No surprise fees — gym owners across dozens of reviews cite "unexpected processing fees" and "hidden costs" as top reasons for switching platforms

**What they hate:**
- Platforms that bury processing fees in fine print, then charge them separately from the subscription cost
- Autopay setups that require multiple steps or support tickets to configure
- Billing that fails silently — a member's card declines and the system doesn't alert anyone
- Inability to give partial refunds or apply credits without calling support
- Annual contracts with no early exit — this is cited as a dealbreaker in Mindbody reviews specifically, where owners describe feeling "trapped"

**The gap TGP can fill:** Full billing transparency in one screen. Show the owner exactly what TGP's 3.3% GMV fee costs them in real dollars, right on the dashboard. No mystery. Owners who trust the math trust the platform.

---

## 2. Member Management and Retention Intelligence

Gym owners think in two modes: growing the membership base and keeping the members they have. Software needs to serve both, but most tools do acquisition badly and retention even worse.

**What they want:**
- A complete member profile that includes: membership type, join date, billing status, check-in history, class attendance, linked trainer, notes, emergency contact, signed waivers, and communication history — all on one screen
- Churn prediction: which members are at risk of cancelling before they actually cancel. Wodify's "Retain" module is the most praised feature in boutique gym reviews specifically because it surfaces at-risk members based on attendance drop-off patterns
- Automated win-back campaigns: when a member goes 7+ days without checking in, the system should be able to automatically trigger an SMS or email without the owner having to notice it manually
- Member lifetime value (LTV) tracking — owners want to know which members are high-value so they can treat them accordingly
- Attendance reports by class, by day, by time slot — to understand what's working and fill dead hours
- The ability to tag or segment members (e.g., "referred by Instagram," "at-risk," "high-value," "corporate account") for targeted outreach

**What they hate:**
- Member data spread across multiple screens or tools — having to open billing in one tab and check-in history in another
- Manual processes for following up with lapsed members — if the software doesn't automate it, it doesn't get done
- No way to see which classes are underperforming — just a list of attendance numbers with no context or comparison
- Reporting that gives raw numbers with no trend lines, comparisons, or actionable context

**The gap TGP can fill:** A risk board that is visible from the owner's home screen — not buried in a reports section. Three columns: Red (lapsed 14+ days), Amber (lapsed 7–13 days), Green (active). One tap to send a check-in message. This is the feature owners describe wanting the most and finding the least.

---

## 3. Scheduling and Class Management

Scheduling is the operational backbone of every gym. It's also one of the most consistently complained-about areas across all platforms.

**What they want:**
- A calendar view that shows every class, every time slot, every trainer, and fill rates at a glance
- Easy setup for recurring class schedules — set it once, it runs every week until changed
- Waitlist management that automatically promotes the next person when a spot opens, and notifies them instantly
- Booking limits per class, per member type, per time window (e.g., members can book only 24 hours in advance)
- The ability to substitute a coach on a class without cancelling and recreating it
- Class templates — instead of building the same Monday/Wednesday/Friday schedule from scratch each week, owners want to copy and apply
- Appointment scheduling for 1:1 personal training sessions that integrates with class schedules so trainers aren't double-booked
- Online booking embedded on the gym's own website or landing page — not just through the platform's consumer app

**What they hate:**
- Systems that require 5+ taps to make a simple schedule change
- No ability to add a walk-in to a class at the door without breaking the booking system
- Calendar views that don't show capacity at a glance — just a list of class names with no visual fill indicator
- Rescheduling a session requiring full cancellation and re-creation instead of a drag-and-drop or one-tap edit

**The gap TGP can fill:** Color-coded class cards showing fill percentage visually (green at 50%, yellow at 80%, red at 100%), with a waitlist count shown on the card. No clicks needed to see the health of your schedule.

---

## 4. Business Analytics and Reporting

This is the most common source of "missing features" complaints across all major platforms. Gym owners are running small businesses and need business intelligence, not just raw data.

**What they want:**
- Revenue trend over time: month over month, year over year, compared to the same period last year
- Member count changes: new joins, cancellations, net growth — as a trend, not just a current snapshot
- Class fill rate analytics: which classes are always full (demand exceeds supply), which are always empty (should be cut or moved)
- Trainer performance: how many sessions has each trainer delivered? What is their client retention rate? What GMV are they generating?
- Revenue breakdown by source: memberships vs. session packs vs. drop-ins vs. retail — so owners know where their money actually comes from
- Lead-to-member conversion tracking: how many leads came in this month, how many converted, what was the average time to conversion
- Custom date ranges — not just preset "this month" or "last 30 days" but any arbitrary date window the owner needs
- Export to CSV or PDF for accountants, investors, or personal records

**What they hate:**
- Canned reports with no customization — Mindbody receives the most complaints about this specifically. Owners describe needing to pull data into Excel and build their own charts because the platform's reports are fixed and generic
- No way to compare two time periods side by side
- Reports that show totals but not trends — knowing you have 312 members today is less useful than knowing you had 290 last month and 260 the month before
- Data that's accurate at the report level but inconsistent at the individual member level

**The gap TGP can fill:** A custom report builder with a drag-and-drop interface — pick your metrics, pick your date range, pick your grouping. Save it as a template. Schedule it as a weekly email to yourself. This alone is worth the price of the platform for a serious gym owner.

---

## 5. Staff and Trainer Management

Once a gym has more than one or two employees, managing staff becomes its own full-time job. Most platforms treat staff management as a secondary feature — it's not.

**What they want:**
- Role-based permissions: the owner sees everything, the manager sees most things, the trainer sees only their clients and schedule, the front desk sees check-in and billing basics
- Staff scheduling and availability management — trainers should be able to set their available hours, and the system should prevent double-booking automatically
- Coach commission tracking: for every PT session delivered, track what the trainer earns and what the gym keeps. This should calculate automatically based on pre-set commission rates
- Staff performance dashboards: sessions delivered, attendance rates, client reviews, revenue generated — visible to the owner at a glance
- The ability to add a new staff member in minutes with pre-set role templates, not a support ticket
- Communication tools: internal messaging or announcements between staff members

**What they hate:**
- Having to build commission calculations in a spreadsheet because the software doesn't do it
- Staff members being able to see member billing information they shouldn't have access to
- No visibility into whether trainers are actually showing up and delivering the sessions on the schedule
- Flat "admin" vs. "non-admin" permission models with no granularity

**The gap TGP can fill:** A trainer P&L view for the owner — per trainer, per month: sessions delivered, revenue generated, platform cut, trainer payout, client retention rate. One screen, all the data. No spreadsheets.

---

## 6. Marketing, Lead Management, and Growth Tools

Most gym owners are not marketers. They need software that acts like a marketing team for them — capturing leads, following up automatically, and converting trials to full members.

**What they want:**
- A simple CRM for tracking leads: name, contact info, source (Instagram, Google, referral, walk-in), status (new lead, contacted, trial scheduled, converted), and notes
- Automated follow-up sequences: when a new lead fills out a form, the system automatically sends a welcome message, then a follow-up after 24 hours, then a reminder after 3 days — without the owner having to do any of it
- Trial membership management: prospects on a free trial are tracked separately, with automatic conversion nudges as the trial end date approaches
- Referral tracking: which current members are sending new members? The platform should track this and enable referral reward programs
- Email and SMS campaigns: the ability to send targeted messages to specific segments (e.g., "all members who haven't checked in for 10 days" or "all members on a 10-session pack with fewer than 3 sessions remaining")
- Integration with Instagram, Google, and Facebook for lead capture from ads directly into the CRM

**What they hate:**
- A CRM that requires manual data entry for every lead
- No automation — every follow-up is a task the owner has to remember to do
- Generic blast emails with no segmentation — messaging every member the same thing regardless of their status
- Marketing tools that are add-ons at extra cost rather than built in

**The gap TGP can fill:** A visual automation builder. If/then workflows with no code required. "If member has not checked in for 7 days → send SMS → wait 2 days → if no response → send email with offer → notify owner." This is the feature that turns a gym management tool into a retention machine.

---

## 7. Transparent, Predictable Pricing

This deserves its own section because it is one of the top 3 reasons gym owners switch platforms.

**What they want:**
- One price that includes the features they actually use — not a base price that grows with add-ons for mobile apps, automated billing, reporting, and SMS
- No per-member fees — as the gym grows, the software cost should not grow proportionally
- Month-to-month contracts — or at minimum, a clear and fair exit process
- Processing fees that are flat-rate and disclosed upfront, not variable and discovered in the first statement

**What they hate:**
- Mindbody's pricing model is the single most complained-about aspect across all gym software reviews. Owners describe starting at a base rate and ending up paying 3–5x more after add-ons, processing fees, and service charges compound
- Annual contracts with no refund or exit clause
- Pricing that doesn't scale fairly — platforms that charge per member are penalizing the gym for growing

**The TGP model implication:** The free + 3.3% GMV model is exactly what this segment wants. No monthly fee removes the "why am I paying $200 for a half-empty gym" problem. The percentage model means TGP wins when the gym wins. The key is to make the fee calculation fully transparent — show it on the dashboard in real dollars so owners never feel surprised.

---

## 8. Customer Support That Actually Helps

Support quality is the second-most-cited factor in switching decisions (after pricing).

**What they want:**
- Fast response times — gym owners deal with member-facing problems in real time; a 48-hour ticket response is completely unusable when a member is standing at the front desk
- Support staff who understand the fitness industry — not generic SaaS support agents reading from a script
- Multiple contact channels: chat, phone, and email, with chat being the preferred first contact
- Proactive support: when something goes wrong (billing failure, integration bug, data sync error), the platform should detect it and alert the owner before they have to discover it themselves

**What they hate:**
- Inconsistent answers from different support reps — this is the #1 specific complaint in Mindbody reviews, where owners describe calling back and getting a different answer each time
- Support that only troubleshoots and never teaches — owners want to learn how to solve problems themselves, not be dependent on support calls
- Long hold times or email-only support
- Being charged for support or onboarding

# PART 2: WHAT FRONT DESK STAFF WANT

## Overview

Front desk staff are one of the most overlooked personas in gym software design. They are not the buyer (that's the owner) and they are not the end user (that's the member). They are the operators — the people who live in the software every single day, handling an average of 30–80 member interactions per shift, each of which needs to be resolved in under 60 seconds.

The fundamental design constraint for front desk UX is this: **the front desk is not a desk — it is a performance.** A receptionist at a gym is simultaneously greeting members by name, answering the phone, managing a line of people waiting to check in, monitoring class start times, and handling payment disputes. The software has to work in that environment. It has to be fast, obvious, and fault-tolerant.

Every second of confusion or extra tap is a second of a member standing at the counter feeling ignored.

---

## 1. Check-In That Takes Under 10 Seconds

The check-in workflow is the single most-used feature of any gym management platform from the front desk's perspective. It needs to be frictionless.

**What they want:**
- Multiple check-in methods that work interchangeably: member scans a QR code on their phone, taps an NFC card/fob, or gives their name — any of these should trigger an instant green screen confirmation
- Facial recognition check-in as an option for high-volume gyms where queues form at the door
- The check-in screen should be the default screen — not buried two or three menus deep
- Instant membership status display: when a member checks in, the screen immediately shows their name, membership type, expiry date, and any flags (expired, balance due, waiver missing) — all without any additional clicks
- A check-in kiosk mode that members can self-serve, freeing the front desk from doing it manually at all
- Check-in should work offline or in degraded connectivity — gym check-in is not something that can be blocked by a slow internet connection

**What they hate:**
- Multi-step check-in flows that require the staff member to navigate through menus
- Systems that show member names but require a second click to confirm them — every extra click multiplies across hundreds of interactions per day
- No visual indicator of membership status — staff having to read text carefully to determine if a member is active or expired
- Systems that go down during peak morning hours because of server issues or required updates

**The TGP design solution:** Full-screen check-in with color coding. Green = active member, check them in. Yellow = note (e.g., session pack running low, waiver about to expire). Red = action required (expired membership, outstanding balance). No reading required — staff process the color and either wave the member through or say "Hey, quick note on your account."

---

## 2. One-Screen Member View

When a member has a question, a complaint, or a problem, front desk staff need to answer it immediately — not put the member on hold while they navigate through multiple screens.

**What they want:**
- A single member profile accessible from the check-in screen with one tap — showing: membership status, billing history, class bookings, session pack balance, trainer assignment, signed waivers, and any notes left by the owner or manager
- The ability to process common transactions directly from the member profile: sell a session pack, process a payment, update a payment method, apply a credit — without navigating away
- A notes field that staff can update with context about the member — "asked about upgrading in July," "knee injury — inform trainer," "referring friend next week"
- Call-out alerts that appear automatically when a member's record has something important: their membership expires in 3 days, they have an unsigned waiver, their recurring payment failed last week

**What they hate:**
- Having to open billing in a separate module from the member profile
- No way to see a member's upcoming bookings from the check-in screen — staff are constantly asked "am I booked for Thursday?" and have to navigate to a calendar to answer
- Notes fields that exist but don't display prominently — the note was written by the manager but the front desk staff never see it
- Slow load times on member profiles — if the profile takes 3 seconds to load, every interaction with a member is delayed

---

## 3. Walk-In and Day Pass Handling

Not every person who walks through the door is a member. Front desk staff regularly handle walk-ins who want a day pass, guests of members, or prospects who want a tour. These workflows need to be just as smooth as member check-in.

**What they want:**
- A "sell day pass" button that is visible from the main check-in screen — not hidden in a POS sub-menu
- The ability to register a guest in under 60 seconds: name, email, payment — done
- Digital waiver collection for guests that can be signed on a tablet or phone on the spot
- Prospect intake: when someone comes in for a tour, staff can log them as a lead with contact info and source in seconds, and the system routes that lead into the CRM automatically
- A guest log so the owner can see how many walk-ins and prospects came in each day

**What they hate:**
- Day pass sales that require navigating to a completely separate POS workflow, losing the check-in screen state
- Paper waivers for guests when digital waivers exist for members
- No way to track walk-in volume or conversion from walk-in to member
- Guest check-in creating phantom records in the member database that then have to be cleaned up

---

## 4. Point of Sale (POS) for Retail and Session Sales

Most gyms sell more than memberships. Protein shakes, branded gear, session packs, and class credits are all common front-desk transactions. The POS needs to feel like a retail tool, not an afterthought.

**What they want:**
- A simple, fast POS that can process retail sales, session pack purchases, and class credits in the same workflow — not three separate modules
- Pre-loaded product catalog with prices, so staff don't have to remember or look up pricing
- The ability to charge to a member's card on file with one tap — no re-entering payment information
- Cash handling support: record cash payments, open a till, reconcile at end of shift
- Receipt via email or SMS — no paper receipt printer required
- Inventory tracking for retail items: when protein shake stock runs low, the system flags it

**What they hate:**
- POS that requires logging into a different system or module
- No ability to apply a discount or promo code at the time of sale without manager override
- Charging a card on file requiring the member's physical card — this defeats the purpose of having it on file
- No sales history for the shift — staff can't answer "what did I sell today?"

---

## 5. Class and Schedule Visibility

The front desk is the first person members ask about classes — what's available, who's teaching, how many spots are left, whether the waitlist is moving. Staff need real-time class visibility without leaving the check-in screen.

**What they want:**
- A live class dashboard showing today's schedule: class name, start time, trainer, booked count, capacity, and waitlist count — all at a glance
- The ability to manually add a member to a class from the front desk (walk-in who forgot to book)
- Waitlist management that staff can operate: manually move someone from the waitlist to a spot, or remove someone from the list
- Class cancellation alerts: if a class is cancelled last-minute, staff need to know so they can tell arriving members
- A view of tomorrow's classes as well as today — members arriving for a morning session often ask about classes later that day or the next morning

**What they hate:**
- Class schedule that is only accessible from a separate calendar view — staff have to navigate away from check-in to answer "is there a 6pm class tonight?"
- No real-time capacity counter — staff are guessing at whether a class is full
- Waitlist management that requires calling or texting members manually when a spot opens — this should be fully automated

---

## 6. Handling Alerts and Exceptions Without Stress

Some of the most stressful moments in a front desk shift happen when something is wrong with a member's account — an expired membership, a failed payment, an unsigned waiver — and the member is standing there expecting to be waved through.

**What they want:**
- Soft alerts vs. hard blocks: staff need to be able to distinguish between "this member has an overdue balance — mention it but let them in" vs. "this member's account is suspended — do not allow access"
- A script suggestion built into the alert: when a flag appears, the system suggests what to say. E.g., "Hi [Name], quick note — your membership renewed but the payment didn't go through. Do you want to update your card now or after class?"
- The ability to override or dismiss an alert with a reason logged — sometimes the manager has already handled something and the front desk shouldn't re-raise it
- Clear visual hierarchy: red for blockers, yellow for things to mention, grey for informational only
- Auto-resolution: when a member updates their card on file through the member app, the payment failure flag should clear automatically without front desk intervention

**What they hate:**
- Alerts that don't tell staff what to do — just a warning message with no guidance
- Alerts that can't be dismissed — front desk staff describe being forced to click through the same expired credit card warning 5 times in a row before being allowed to check the member in
- Alerts that appear with no context — "Account issue" with no detail is useless when a member is standing there asking what's wrong

---

## 7. Speed Under Pressure

This is the meta-requirement that underlies everything else. Front desk software is used during the highest-stress periods of the day: the 6am rush when 40 members show up in 15 minutes, the lunchtime spike, and the post-work surge. The software must perform under pressure.

**What they want:**
- Load times under 1 second for every interaction — check-in confirmation, member profile load, payment processing
- No required software updates during business hours
- Offline mode for check-in: if the internet goes down, check-ins should still be logged locally and synced when connection is restored
- A UI that works equally well on a tablet mounted at the front desk and on a laptop behind the counter
- Large tap targets — front desk staff are often checking people in while standing up, and small buttons cause errors
- Keyboard shortcuts for power users — staff who work the front desk 5 days a week will learn keyboard shortcuts if they exist; they dramatically speed up common tasks

**What they hate:**
- Pages that require scrolling to reach important buttons
- Confirmation dialogs on obvious actions — asking "are you sure you want to check in this member?" adds time to every single transaction
- Buttons that look the same for actions with very different consequences (e.g., "check in" and "cancel membership" styled identically)
- Session timeouts that log staff out in the middle of a shift

---

## 8. Training New Staff in Under 30 Minutes

Front desk is often the highest-turnover role in a gym. The software needs to be learnable by someone with zero prior experience in gym management tools — in a single shift.

**What they want:**
- A UI so clear that the most common workflows are discoverable without training — the primary action on every screen should be the most visually prominent element
- Contextual help: when a new staff member hovers over an unfamiliar button or field, a one-sentence explanation appears
- A "new staff" checklist or guided walkthrough that covers the 5 things front desk staff do 90% of the time: check in a member, sell a day pass, add someone to a class, handle a payment issue, look up a member
- Role-based view that hides settings and features front desk staff will never use — seeing the full platform is overwhelming and creates confusion

**What they hate:**
- Software that requires a 2-hour training session before a new hire can do anything useful
- Features and menus visible to front desk staff that they are not authorized to use — this creates confusion and accidental mistakes
- No in-app help — staff have to leave the software and search online to answer basic questions
- Settings that are easy to accidentally change from the front desk screen

---

## Summary: The Core Principle for Each Persona

**Gym owners** need software that gives them **control and clarity** — control over their revenue, their members, and their team; and clarity on what's working, what's breaking, and what to do next. They are time-poor and trust-sensitive. Win their trust with transparency and save their time with automation.

**Front desk staff** need software that gives them **speed and confidence** — speed to get through a 40-person rush without a queue forming, and confidence that the information on screen is accurate and actionable. They are not evaluating the software; they are executing with it. Every second of confusion is a second a member spends waiting.

Build for both and you have a platform that the owner buys *and* the staff actually use.
